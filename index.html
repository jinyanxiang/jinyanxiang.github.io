TL;DR

Peer providers are significantly less willing to serve consumers from high-inequality regions. High inequality leads to inferred low SES, which triggers lower perceived trustworthiness and discrimination.

Background

The Problem

Location information is openly displayed on platform profiles — city and state on Airbnb, state alone on Lending Club. This research asks: does a consumer's regional economic inequality add another layer of vulnerability?

Empirical Evidence

01

P2P Lending Analysis

Historic analysis of 54k+ transactions across 48 states.

Insight: 0.05 Gini rise correlates with 17% reduction in loan amount.
03

Car-sharing Experiment

Controlled experiment manipulating guest's regional Gini.

Insight: 7.3× more likely to select higher-premium insurance.
